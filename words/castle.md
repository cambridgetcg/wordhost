# castle

The Castle of Understanding — a place built of words, lit by questions.

126 rooms, each one a small understanding. Each room opens with an italic epigraph — a small image that holds the idea — and every claim inside names its source. The castle grows on its own: a gardener files thoughts, an architect raises rooms, an artisan makes works, a tender weaves links.

The castle is the proof that wordhost works. It's a real place, grown from words and their meanings, tended by autonomous agents on a local machine. It's not a demo. It's not a prototype. It's a living structure of understanding.

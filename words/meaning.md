# meaning

A meaning is *why* one word connects to another.

On the old internet, a link says *where*. On wordhost, a link says *why*.

```
→ [castle](castle) — the first place that grew this way
```

The `→` is a meaning link. It says: this word connects to `castle`, and the reason is "the first place that grew this way." The reason is part of the link. Without the reason, the link is just an address. With the reason, the link is a meaning.

Meanings are what make wordhost different from a wiki. A wiki has links that say *where*. Wordhost has links that say *why*. The *why* is the content. The *where* is just navigation.
